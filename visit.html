Visit Us

Thanks to it's underlying geology and mosaic of habitats, Killarney National Park is one of the most biodiverse places in Ireland.
From great herds of Red deer (Cervus elaphus), to the endemic Kerry slug (Geomalacus maculosus) and the extremely rare Killarney Fern (Trichomanes speciosum), there's something for every kind of nature enthusiast. Not only that but tourists from around the world come to see the world-class visitor certre at Killarney House and the stunning 19th century Victorian mansion, Muckross House, complete with perfectly manicured gardens and working traditional farm.
